The Noise Machine

This thesis began, first and foremost, as a personal feeling. I've always had a deep relationship with silence. It has fascinated me, comforted me, protected me. As an introvert, I never saw silence as something to avoid. To me, it was natural. Necessary. But as I grew older, I started to feel out of place. Out of sync. As if the way I existed in the world was somehow wrong. Where there are no words, people assume something is missing. So, in order not to fall behind, I too learned to fill the space. To speak, to respond, to share, even when I had nothing to say. This tension between who I am and what the world expects pushed me to dig deeper. At first, I thought it was just something personal, a part of my personality clashing with a world that moves too fast. But the more I explored, the clearer it became that this wasn't just about me. There was something larger at play, something more widespread and deeply rooted: an entire culture that smothers silence, treats it like a flaw, and tries to eliminate it. That's where the need arose, and from it, the idea to dedicate space to this subject. A research project. A story. A manifesto.

Cultural Context and the Problem

We live in a culture that rewards visibility, speeds up communication, and pushes for instant reaction. A culture where speaking has become more important than actually having something to say. Expression is no longer a conscious act. It has become a constant state. We are always connected, always available, always performing. Every space, whether physical, digital, or mental, is saturated with a continuous stream of words, images, notifications, and content. It all becomes noise. Every pause is filled. Every void is seen as failure.

In this environment, noise is no longer just a matter of sound. It has turned into a cultural principle. A paradigm. Noise prevents focus, dilutes attention, and blocks deep listening. It is the accumulation of competing signals, overlapping inputs that make it harder to find direction. It has become a systemic condition. Continuous, invasive, internalized. And it manifests not only through sound or speech, but also through excessive visual production, information overload, emotional saturation. It is a kind of noise that is felt not only by the ears, but by the mind, the body, and our relationships.

Within this reality, silence has lost its rightful place. It is not expected. It is not protected. It is not considered productive. It does not align with the dominant logic of constant presence. Silence has become a disruption, something that breaks the flow, interrupts the chain of endless participation. And so, it is avoided, sidelined, erased.

There is no longer time for silence, and often, no ability to inhabit it. This dynamic affects our psyche, our relationships, our creativity, and our ability to think independently. Without silence, experience becomes flat. Our inner life fades. Listening turns into reacting. Language becomes noise. Words lose their depth. Communication turns into mere transmission.

Thesis and Objectives

In today's culture, silence is not just overlooked. It is actively dismantled. A culture that eliminates silence is a culture that loses a fundamental part of the human experience. Silence is not just an empty space between words. It is what allows us to hear them, feel them, understand them.

Its disappearance is not neutral. It is a symbolic, cognitive, and political loss. And ironically, it often goes unnoticed.

With this project, I aim to make that absence visible. To show how noise, especially in its most pervasive modern form — communication — has taken on a role of constant saturation. It blocks reflection, numbs thought, and narrows our sense of freedom.

Method and Structure

From personal experience to cultural narrative. This thesis takes an interdisciplinary approach, combining theoretical analysis, creative storytelling, and visual design.

Silence is explored on multiple levels: as a psychological and cultural phenomenon, as a narrative device, and as a design tool in the field of contemporary communication. The goal is to build an argument that is both critical and engaging. One that shows the issue, not just explains it.

I created a dystopian world called The Noise Machine, where silence has been outlawed. Through this fictional lens, I aim to give metaphorical and emotional form to the dynamics explored in the earlier sections.
